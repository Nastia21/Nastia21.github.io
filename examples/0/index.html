Wild animals of our planet

The main difference between vertebrates and invertebrates is the presence of a cartilaginous or bony skeleton. The basis of the skeleton is the spine - hence the name - vertebrates. Many animals of this class have strong muscles, many have highly developed sense organs. The main representatives of the class of vertebrates are mammals, fish, birds, amphibians and reptiles. The ancestors of vertebrates, according to scientists, resemble modern lancelets.

In Thailand, there are up to 75 species of lizards and about 100 species of snakes, 13 of which are venomous, including the king cobra. Crocodiles are represented by Siamese and combed crocodiles, until 1970 the gavial crocodile also lived in Thailand. The rivers of Thailand are rich in fish, mainly of the carp family. On the coast of the Gulf of Thailand, herring and mackerel are mined, sharks are hunted.
